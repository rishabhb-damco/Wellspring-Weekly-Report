Why Did the May Scheduling Rate Drop?
Source mix analysis — the rate decline is not a Google or channel problem. It is a volume and quality shift in Internal and Referral leads.
Root Cause #1
18 → 4
Internal leads collapsed

Internal leads (word-of-mouth, existing patients) have a 94% scheduling rate. April had 18 — May had only 4. That alone removed ~13 scheduled consultations from the pipeline.

Root Cause #2
67% → 25%
Referral quality deteriorated

Referral leads had a 67% scheduling rate in April. In May that fell to 25% — only 2 of 8 referrals scheduled. This may indicate warmer referrals (existing connections) were replaced by more distant ones.

Google's Role
Stable
Not the cause of the drop

Google leads went from 44% scheduling in April to 30% in May — a modest dip, largely because volume fell (25 → 10 leads). The Google ads performance itself is not the driver of the overall rate decline.

Key Insights & Recommendations
What the data tells us about Google lead quality and channel strategy
52%

Google Phone Calls Convert at 52%

Despite coming from paid search, Google phone call leads schedule at 52% — well above Google form leads (30%). Every inbound Google call that reaches intake is a high-value touchpoint worth prioritising.

30%

Google Form Leads Leave 70% Unconverted

76% of Google leads arrive via form, but only 30% schedule. This is the single biggest conversion gap in the funnel. Faster follow-up, better nurturing sequences, or a call-back prompt could recapture a significant portion of these leads.

78%

March Google Phone Rate — Best on Record

7 of 9 Google phone call leads scheduled in March 2026. If intake handling was consistent in March, that approach should be documented and used as the standard process going forward.

57%

Google Drives Over Half of All Form Leads

57% of all form submissions across the practice come from Google. Improving Google form-to-schedule conversion even by 10 percentage points would add roughly 8–9 additional scheduled consultations per month at current volume.

Recommendation: Two-Track Action Plan

For Google phone leads: Ensure calls are answered immediately. Each missed call from Google costs ~$50 in ad spend. Set up a same-day callback system.

For Google form leads: Add an automated SMS within 15 minutes of form submission. Forms that don't get an immediate response are unlikely to convert — 70% currently don't.
