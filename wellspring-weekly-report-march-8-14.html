Key Observations
Conversions are trending in the right direction. We moved from 10 to 11 conversions week-on-week (+10%) without increasing spend. The campaigns are becoming more efficient and we will continue to optimise toward this trajectory.
DSA is our strongest performer this week. We are seeing a cost per conversion of $22.60 on DSA vs. $112.74 on Search, with a CTR of 8.33% confirming the intent match is working well. We will be looking to shift more budget weight here in the coming week.
4 leads were lost to factors outside the campaign. These dropped off due to service gaps — ESA letters not offered and no provider available. We cannot recover these through ad changes alone. We would recommend the Wellspring team reviews capacity and service scope as a parallel action.
Psychotherapy is driving most inbound volume at 52%. We plan to test copy and creative assets that redirect condition-specific queries (ADHD, Anxiety, ADD) toward medication management. These conditions tend to convert at higher value and we want to shift the inbound mix accordingly.
2 leads went uncontacted after the consult call. We know the Wellspring team has a follow-up process in place — the question is how we can make it work harder together. Whether that's tightening the timing, refining the messaging, or aligning it with what we're seeing on the ad side, we'd like to explore what's working and where we can improve the re-contact rate.
Actions and Recommendations
1
Confirmation needed: Does HAP fall under non-accepted insurance? We are seeing HAP-related leads not converting, which is contributing to 20% of lost leads. Once confirmed, we will add HAP as a negative targeting signal across both campaigns to stop spend on ineligible leads.
2
We will increase budget allocation toward DSA. At $22.60 CPA and 8.33% CTR, DSA is significantly outperforming Search. We recommend shifting a portion of the Search budget to DSA this week to drive more volume at a lower cost per lead.
3
How can we make the missed-call follow-up process more effective? With 2 leads uncontacted this week, we'd like to work with the Wellspring team to review what's currently in place and identify whether adjustments to timing, channel, or messaging could improve the re-contact rate.
4
We will add ESA-related negative keywords to both campaigns. Searches for ESA letters are reaching the form but cannot be served. We will suppress these upstream by adding terms like "ESA letter" and "emotional support animal letter" as negatives to reduce wasted spend.
5
Let's look at the re-engagement sequence together. We can bring in what we're seeing from the ad side — intent signals, query patterns, peak response windows — to help sharpen the timing and messaging for better conversion on missed calls.
6
We will build out condition-specific ad copy and landing page assets. Our recommendation is to lead with ADHD, Anxiety, and ADD messaging, as these conditions convert naturally into medication management. We will draft new copy variants and share for review before going live.
