Key Observations
Best scheduling week on record for Google leads. 2 of 2 Google-sourced leads confirmed appointments (100%), outperforming the April 12–18 period's 50% rate. Without increasing spend, quality of inbound is improving.
DSA is our strongest performer this period. Cost per conversion of $27.02 on DSA vs. $63.87 on Search. DSA phone conversions (5) are driving the bulk of pipeline — strong intent match validated by 5.97% CTR.
Spend is at $263 with 7 total platform conversions. CPA of $37.55 is well below the $100 benchmark. Budget utilisation was conservative mid-week (Apr 28 DSA had 0 clicks) — there may be room to increase daily caps on DSA.
Psychotherapy is driving 63.6% of inbound volume. Medication Management accounts for 36.4%. We plan to test copy and bidding strategies that direct ADHD, ADD, and Depression queries toward medication management leads.
One lead had an incomplete inquiry (form abandonment). A minor was also flagged as unable to be served for medication management. These are the only 2 unscheduled leads — both non-Google sourced.
Actions and Recommendations
1
Increase DSA daily budget cap. At $27.02 CPA and 5.97% CTR, DSA is significantly outperforming Search. Reallocating $20–$30/day from Search to DSA is recommended immediately.
2
Add "minor" and child/paediatric keywords as negatives. One unscheduled lead was a minor seeking medication management — a service not offered. Adding these negatives will reduce unqualified spend.
3
Add ESA-related negative keywords. Continuing prior recommendation — "ESA letter", "emotional support animal" as broad negatives to reduce wasted impressions on searches we cannot serve.
4
Investigate form abandonment recovery. One incomplete inquiry was followed up on May 4. Implement a post-submission SMS reminder within 30 minutes of partial form completion to recover these leads.
5
Build condition-specific ad copy for Medication Management. As psychotherapy capacity fills, routing ADHD/ADD/Depression copy toward medication management will improve intake balance and reduce waitlist pressure.
