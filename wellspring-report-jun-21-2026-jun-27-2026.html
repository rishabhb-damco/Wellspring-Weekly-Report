Key Observations
The delivery conflict has been resolved. For the first time in several weeks, both the DSA and Broad Match campaigns ran on all 7 days without alternating. This is a meaningful operational improvement — the account is now running as intended and should produce more consistent results going forward.
This week’s numbers are incomplete — most “not booked” leads are still in progress. 5 of the 8 Google leads are actively being followed up, not definitively lost. The $81 CPL and 37.5% booking rate reflect the current state, not the final outcome. Once follow-ups close over the next few days, both metrics are likely to improve significantly.
Platform activity hit a new high — 16 total actions in one week. 6 form submissions and 10 phone calls generated from $645 in ad spend represents the strongest platform conversion volume we have seen. This is a genuine signal that ad reach and relevance are improving, even if the CRM numbers are lagging behind.
What’s Next
🔍 Exploring other platforms to scale leads
Google Ads is generating strong platform activity but has a ceiling given current search demand trends. We are actively evaluating Meta (Facebook & Instagram), Psychology Today premium positioning, and YouTube pre-roll as additional patient acquisition channels. A multi-platform approach would reduce over-reliance on search and open Wellspring to audiences who are not yet actively searching but are receptive to therapy services.
